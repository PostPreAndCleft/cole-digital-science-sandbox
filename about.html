About

My research uncovers the hidden organizational strategies that allow synapses to structurally achieve the balance that makes learning and memory possible.

Research overview

Using electron tomography, I revealed that synaptic vesicles are not free-floating but tethered into dense clouds near the active zone—an organization hidden by traditional electron microscopy. Where vesicles were once thought to drift ungovernably by diffusion, we now know molecular tethers organize them. These tethers offer points of control.

This is the template for my work toward clinical relevance: identify the structural "handles" biology uses to control stochastic nanoscale processes, and understand how their disruption contributes to pathology. By revealing how these physical control points fail, we can better address the structural underpinnings of disorders like Alzheimer's disease and related dementias.
